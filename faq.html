The Piercing Experience

We specialize exclusively in earlobe piercings — single, pair, second lobe, third lobe, and stacked lobe looks. We do not offer cartilage, nose, lip, navel, or other body piercings. Our singular focus on lobe piercings means we do this one thing exceptionally well, with the right tool, the right jewelry, and the right expertise every single time.

First: it is not a piercing gun. A traditional spring-loaded gun uses uncontrolled force, cannot be fully sterilized between clients, and causes unnecessary blunt trauma to tissue.

The Inverness System is a hand-pressured piercing instrument — quiet, smooth, and a world apart from the spring-loaded guns you may have experienced elsewhere. Here's what makes it different:

◇ Zero contamination: The jewelry arrives pre-loaded in a sealed, single-use sterile cartridge. It goes directly from that cartridge into your ear — never touched, never transferred.

◇ Full piercer control: Unlike spring-loaded guns, the speed and pressure are entirely in Holly's hands at every moment — for precise, intentional placement.

◇ Right tool for the job: Needles are designed for cartilage and body piercings. For soft earlobe tissue, they're overkill. Our device is purpose-built for lobe piercings — one clean, one-step motion with no extra instrument handling.

◇ Safe for all ages: Including infants 6 months and older. The process is fast, gentle, and over in seconds.

The result? A safer, cleaner, more precise piercing experience than what you've likely had anywhere else.

The Inverness System is quiet, gentle, and designed to minimize discomfort — most people report feeling no discomfort at all. We pierce with three body-safe materials: implant-grade titanium (nickel-free), solid 14K gold, and medical-grade stainless steel that meets the most stringent global standards for nickel release requirements. All are appropriate for fresh piercings and sourced from trusted vendors with material certifications. We do not use mystery alloys, plated jewelry, or acrylic. If you have known metal sensitivities, let us know at your consultation and we'll recommend the best option for your body.

Once your piercing is fully healed, you're welcome to come in for a jewelry change with a piece of your own — we're happy to swap it safely.

Healing & Aftercare

Earlobe piercings typically take 6–9 months to fully heal — though they often feel comfortable much sooner. Healing is a process, not an event. Even when a lobe feels fine, the tissue continues to mature internally for months. Holly will give you personalized guidance at your appointment.

We recommend sterile saline wound wash (like NeilMed Wound Wash) once or twice daily. Spray directly onto the piercing and let it air dry. That's it — simple is better.

Things to avoid: touching the piercing with unwashed hands, rotating or twisting the jewelry, sleeping directly on the piercing, submerging in pools/hot tubs/lakes/open water, using alcohol, hydrogen peroxide, or tea tree oil. Don't over-clean — less is more.

Wait until your piercing is fully healed — even if it looks and feels fine on the outside, the internal tissue takes longer to fully mature. We strongly recommend coming in for a professional jewelry change rather than doing it yourself. Book a jewelry change appointment and Holly will assess the piercing and swap to your new piece safely.

Don't panic — some redness, tenderness, and clear/white discharge are completely normal, especially in the first few weeks. If you notice unusual swelling, thick yellow or green discharge, significant pain, or a bump that isn't going away, reach out to us right away. We're always here to support your healing journey and would rather you contact us early than wait and wonder.

Quick Reference

Aftercare at a Glance

✓  Do This

  • ◇ Rinse with sterile saline wound wash 1–2x daily
  • ◇ Let the piercing air dry after cleaning
  • ◇ Sleep on a clean pillowcase (silk is ideal)
  • ◇ Keep hair products, perfume, and makeup away from the area
  • ◇ Come back for your downsize at 6–8 weeks
  • ◇ Contact us if anything seems off

✗  Avoid This

  • ◇ Touching the piercing with unwashed hands
  • ◇ Rotating or twisting the jewelry
  • ◇ Alcohol, hydrogen peroxide, or tea tree oil
  • ◇ Pools, hot tubs, lakes, or open water
  • ◇ Sleeping directly on the piercing
  • ◇ Changing jewelry before it's fully healed
