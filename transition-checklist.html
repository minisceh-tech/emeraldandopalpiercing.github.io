PHASE 1 — Export Everything from Wix First

Export Your Contacts

Open your browser and go to wix.com
Click "Log In" in the top right corner
Enter your email address and password
Click on your site to open the dashboard
In the left sidebar, click "CRM" or "Contacts"
Look for an "Import/Export" button near the top of the page
Click "Export Contacts"
When the file downloads, move it to your Desktop and rename it "Wix Contacts.csv"

Export Your Booking History

In the left sidebar, click "Bookings"
Click "Appointments"
Look for an Export or Download button (may be a small icon near the top right)
Download and save to your Desktop, rename it "Wix Bookings.csv"

Export Email Subscribers

In the left sidebar, click "Email Marketing"
Look for "Contacts" or "Subscribers"
Click Export and save to Desktop, rename it "Wix Subscribers.csv"

Export Financial Records

In the left sidebar, click "Finances" or "Payments"
Look for an Export button
Export your transaction history and save to Desktop, rename it "Wix Payments.csv"
💡 Keep these financial records for tax purposes even if you don't import them into Vagaro.

Download Your Photos from Wix

In the left sidebar, click "Media" or "Media Manager"
Press Ctrl+A to select all photos
Click "Download"
Save to a folder on your computer — you likely already have most of these but double check

PHASE 2 — Transfer Your Domain Out of Wix

💡 Start this FIRST because it takes 5–7 days. Your Wix site stays completely live the whole time.

Unlock Your Domain in Wix

In your Wix dashboard, click "Domains" in the left sidebar
Click on emeraldandopalpiercing.com
Look for a "Domain Lock" or "Lock" toggle — make sure it is turned OFF (unlocked)
Click "Transfer away from Wix"
Follow the steps Wix shows you
Check your email — Wix will send you an authorization code called an EPP code
Copy that code and paste it into a Notes app or document — you will need it in the next step

Set Up a Namecheap Account (Your New Domain Home)

Open a new browser tab and go to namecheap.com
Click "Sign Up" in the top right corner
Enter your name, email address, and create a password
Check your email and click the verification link Namecheap sends you
Log back into namecheap.com

Transfer Your Domain to Namecheap

In the top menu, hover over "Domains" and click "Transfer"
Type emeraldandopalpiercing.com in the search box and click the arrow/search button
Click "Transfer" next to your domain
When it asks for the EPP/Authorization code, paste in the code from your email
Add to cart and complete the purchase (approximately $10–15, includes one year of registration)
Check your email — you will receive confirmation emails from both Wix and Namecheap
If either email asks you to "approve" or "confirm" the transfer, click the link to approve it
Wait 5–7 days — you will receive a final email when the transfer is 100% complete
✓ Transfer complete — check Namecheap dashboard and confirm domain shows up under "Domain List"

PHASE 5 — Put Your Website on GitHub (Free Hosting)

Create a GitHub Account

Open a new browser tab and go to github.com
Click "Sign Up" in the top right corner
Enter your email address
Create a password and write it down somewhere safe
Choose a username — something simple like emeraldandopal or hollyemeraldopal
Complete the puzzle to prove you are not a robot
Check your email and click the verification link GitHub sends you
When asked about your plan, choose the free plan

Create a Repository (Where Your Files Live)

Once logged in to GitHub, click the green "New" button or the "+" icon in the top right
Click "New repository"
In the "Repository name" box type exactly: piercing-site
Make sure "Public" is selected (not Private)
Leave everything else as-is and click "Create repository" green button at the bottom

Upload Your Website Files

On the page that appears, look for a link that says "uploading an existing file" and click it
Open File Explorer on your computer (the yellow folder icon in your taskbar)
Navigate to C:\Users\minis\piercing-site
Press Ctrl+A to select all files and folders
Drag all selected files from File Explorer into the GitHub upload window in your browser
Wait for all files to finish uploading — this may take a minute or two with the photos
Scroll down on the GitHub page and click the green "Commit changes" button
Wait about 30 seconds for GitHub to process the files

Turn On GitHub Pages (Makes Your Site Live)

Click "Settings" tab at the top of your repository (looks like a gear icon)
In the left sidebar, scroll down and click "Pages"
Under "Source," click the dropdown that says "None" and select "main"
Click the "Save" button
Wait 2 minutes, then refresh the page
GitHub will show a green box with your site URL — it will look like: https://yourusername.github.io/piercing-site
Click that URL to open your live site

Test Your Site on GitHub

Click through every page — Home, About, Services, Gallery, FAQ, Contact
Confirm all photos are loading
Confirm the booking widget appears on the Contact page
Confirm the contact form appears
Open the URL on your phone and check the mobile layout looks good

PHASE 6 — Connect Your Domain to Your New Website

💡 Only do this after Phase 2 is 100% complete (domain fully transferred to Namecheap) and Phase 5 is fully tested and working.

Tell GitHub Your Domain Name

Log into github.com
Click on your piercing-site repository
Click "Settings" tab
Click "Pages" in the left sidebar
Under "Custom Domain," type: emeraldandopalpiercing.com
Click "Save"
Leave this GitHub tab open — you will need to refer back to it

Update Your DNS Settings in Namecheap

Open a new tab and log into namecheap.com
Click "Domain List" in the left sidebar
Click "Manage" next to emeraldandopalpiercing.com
Click "Advanced DNS" tab
Look for any existing "A Record" entries and delete them (click the trash icon next to each one)
Click "Add New Record" and add these 4 A Records exactly (one at a time):
    Type: A Record  |  Host: @  |  Value: 185.199.108.153  |  TTL: Automatic
    Type: A Record  |  Host: @  |  Value: 185.199.109.153  |  TTL: Automatic
    Type: A Record  |  Host: @  |  Value: 185.199.110.153  |  TTL: Automatic
    Type: A Record  |  Host: @  |  Value: 185.199.111.153  |  TTL: Automatic
Add one CNAME Record: Type: CNAME  |  Host: www  |  Value: yourgithubusername.github.io  |  TTL: Automatic
Click the green checkmark or Save button after each record
Wait — DNS changes take 1–4 hours, sometimes up to 48 hours
Type emeraldandopalpiercing.com in your browser — when your new site loads you are live!

Turn On HTTPS (Free Security Certificate)

Go back to GitHub → Settings → Pages
Check the box that says "Enforce HTTPS"
Wait a few minutes and confirm there is a padlock icon when you visit your site
